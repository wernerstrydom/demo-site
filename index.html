This site documents a homelab, ongoing experiments, and opinions on technology and engineering. The goal is not to provide answers, but to surface trade-offs — the uncomfortable reality that every decision in engineering has a cost, and understanding that cost is the job.

If you are looking for a tutorial that tells you what to do, this is the wrong place. If you are looking for a place that asks why, and what happens when things go wrong, read on.

Philosophy

Most technical writing teaches you what to do. This site tries to teach you how to think about what to do. There is a difference. The first produces engineers who follow recipes. The second produces engineers who can reason under uncertainty.

The homelab exists as a consequence of this philosophy. Running real hardware — including hardware that is end-of-life, architecturally diverse, and resource-constrained — forces you to confront assumptions that modern cloud environments paper over. When your firewall is a repurposed Sophos appliance running Ubuntu on an Intel Atom, you learn what the abstractions are hiding.

On this site

This site is built with progressive enhancement. It works on browsers from the year 2000 onwards. It works without JavaScript. It works without CSS. The content is the point. The technology serves the content, not the other way around.

This is also a deliberate choice with a point: a website that requires a modern browser to read text is a website that has confused its means for its ends. The same confusion appears throughout engineering. Recognising it is the first step.
